HR ANALYTICS DASHBOARD SQL BREAKDOWN (MySQL)

These SQL queries were used to extract and analyze workforce data, enabling the Power BI dashboard to provide actionable insights for strategic HR planning, workforce diversity and retention strategies.

Attrition Rate Calculation

This SQL query calculates the overall attrition rate for the organization. It compares the number of employees who left the company to the total employee count, providing a percentage that reflects the company's turnover rate.

Attrition by Gender

Here, I calculated employee attrition based on gender. This helps to understand if attrition is higher in one gender group compared to the other, aiding in targeted retention strategies for specific demographics.

Departmental Attrition Impact

This query provides insights into how different departments are impacted by attrition. By calculating attrition by department, it reveals the departments facing the highest turnover, helping HR focus on those areas for improvement.

Age Distribution and Attrition

This query analyzes employee attrition by age group, helping to identify whether certain age groups are more prone to leaving the company. It is useful for shaping policies that retain younger or older employees.

Education Background and Attrition

I examined how attrition correlates with education levels. This helps to understand if employees with specific educational backgrounds that is, Bachelors and Masters degree are more likely to leave, offering insights for tailored retention strategies.

Key Takeaway & Next Steps

Attrition impacts certain groups more heavily, especially R&D employees and those with Bachelor degrees, highlighting the need for a targeted data-driven retention strategy. Next steps include investigating R&D and Sales attrition causes, creating department-specific action plans, monitoring job satisfaction quarterly, and launching tailored career growth initiatives for mid-career employees.
