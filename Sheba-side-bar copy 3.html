SHEBA MEDICAL CENTER DASHBOARD

This project visualizes healthcare billing and treatment insights to support smarter financial and clinical decisions

Project Overview

The Sheba Medical Center Dashboard is a healthcare analytics project developed to enhance decision-making within hospital management. Utilizing Microsoft Excel and Power BI, the dashboard provides detailed insights into key operational areas such as billing, treatment costs, departmental efficiency, and patient insurance coverage. By centralizing this data, the tool allows stakeholders—including administrators, finance teams, and department heads—to monitor and assess critical performance metrics in real time.

With an emphasis on transparency and performance, the dashboard supports improved hospital operations and smarter resource allocation. Interactive visual reports make it easy to identify trends, track patient satisfaction levels, and understand revenue sources. Ultimately, this tool empowers healthcare leaders to make informed, data-driven decisions that contribute to better patient care and organizational efficiency.

Financial Insights & Medical Cost Breakdown

This dashboard highlights the financial structure of Sheba Medical Center, offering a detailed view of billing and medical service performance.

  • Overall Financial Overview: The hospital recorded a total billing amount of $3 million, with treatment costs also at $3 million. Medication and room charges accounted for $546,000 and $180,000 respectively.
  • Insurance vs. Out-of-Pocket Payments: Insurance providers covered $2 million in bills, while patients paid $1 million out of pocket, reflecting strong insurance involvement in overall healthcare financing..
  • Top Insurance Providers: Aviva, Axa, and Allianz led in billing amounts with $1.128M, $1.123M, and $1.105M respectively, making them the top contributors among insurance partners..
  • Top Insurance Providers: Aviva, Axa, and Allianz led in billing amounts with $1.128M, $1.123M, and $1.105M respectively, making them the top contributors among insurance partners.
  • Departmental and Diagnostic Insights: Cardiology, Orthopedics, and General Surgery generated the most revenue, while hypertension, appendicitis, asthma, fractures, and migraines were the most commonly billed diagnoses.

Problem Statement & Proposed Solution

Hospitals often face challenges in monitoring financial health and efficiently allocating resources due to fragmented data across various departments. As healthcare costs rise and insurance processes become more complex, having a unified view of financial and procedural performance is essential for effective decision-making.To address this, the Sheba Medical Center Dashboard consolidates billing, diagnosis, treatment, and insurance data into a single interactive platform. It empowers hospital executives to monitor cost drivers and revenue streams, track departmental performance, and optimize resource use. Additionally, the dashboard highlights top-billed procedures and diagnoses, while identifying areas with high out-of-pocket expenses to support better patient financial planning.
